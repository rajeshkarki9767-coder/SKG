Nepal's Winter and Your Skin: What Changes from December to February

Nepal's winter (December–February) creates the harshest conditions for skin of any season. In Kathmandu, temperatures drop to 2–8°C overnight with daytime highs of 12–18°C. Humidity falls to 30–40% — similar to desert conditions. Cold mountain winds channelled through the Kathmandu valley accelerate trans-epidermal water loss (TEWL). Indoor heating — gas or electric — reduces humidity further, to 20–25% in heated rooms.

The combined effect on skin: the lipid barrier (the mortar between skin cell bricks) cracks under repeated temperature cycling between cold outdoor and warm indoor environments; water evaporates from the skin surface faster than it can be replaced; and the acid mantle becomes disrupted, causing increased sensitivity to products that were tolerable in other seasons. The result is the widespread dry, flaky, tight, reactive skin that characterises Kathmandu's winter skin crisis.

How to Switch Your Routine for Winter

Cleanser — Switch to Cream, Drop Foam

Any foaming cleanser — however gentle in summer — strips the skin's already-depleted winter lipid barrier. Switch to CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser (Rs. 1,800 at Bhatbhateni) or Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser (Rs. 750) from October onwards. These cream-based cleansers cleanse without stripping. In the mornings, a water rinse without cleanser is sufficient if your nighttime routine is effective — overnight oil accumulation is much lower in winter than summer.

Toner — Hydrating Only, No Exfoliating Toners Daily

Switch from BHA or glycolic toners to a hydrating toner as your daily product. Klairs Supple Preparation Unscented Toner (Rs. 2,200), Anua Heartleaf 77% Toner (Rs. 2,000), or DIY glycerin toner (10% glycerin in water, Rs. 100 for 200ml from pharmacy). Keep exfoliating toners to maximum twice weekly and always follow with richer moisturiser than usual.

Serum — Hyaluronic Acid Applied to Damp Skin

Hyaluronic acid is essential in winter but must be applied correctly. Damp skin → HA serum → immediate moisturiser within 60 seconds. The Ordinary HA 2% + B5 (Rs. 1,400) used twice daily with this technique provides significant improvement in winter dryness within 3–4 days. Layer twice in particularly dry conditions.

Moisturiser — Go Richer

Your monsoon moisturiser is almost certainly insufficient for Nepal's winter. Upgrade: switch from Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel to CeraVe Moisturising Cream (white tub, Rs. 1,900). The ceramide-based thick cream provides the lipid replenishment Nepal's winter strips. Apply within 3 minutes of cleansing. Apply generously — winter is not the time for minimalism with moisturiser.

For very dry skin: add one to two drops of The Ordinary Squalane (Rs. 1,200) to your fingertips, mix with your moisturiser, and apply together. The squalane adds a lipid layer that mimics the skin's natural sebum, significantly improving barrier integrity over three to four weeks of consistent use.

SPF — Still Essential, Choose Moisturising Formula

Many Nepalis skip SPF in winter thinking the weak winter sun does not require it. This is incorrect. Nepal's winter UV is lower than summer but still UV index 3–5 on clear days — enough to cause pigmentation accumulation and collagen degradation over a winter season. Use a moisturising SPF that doubles as your final moisturiser layer: Anua Birch 70 Sun Serum SPF 50+ (Rs. 2,200) or Isntree Hyaluronic Sun Gel SPF 50+ (Rs. 2,000).

Night Routine — Barrier Repair During Sleep

Your skin's repair cycle peaks between 11pm and 4am. In winter, barrier repair during sleep is accelerated by moisturiser + occlusive. The technique: apply CeraVe Moisturising Cream, then apply a very thin film of Vaseline over the driest areas (cheeks, around nose, jaw). This "slugging" approach creates a sealed environment that allows maximum barrier lipid synthesis overnight. Results are visible within 5–7 nights of use.

Essential Winter-Specific Products for Nepal

Facial oil (The Ordinary Squalane, Rs. 1,200): Mix into moisturiser for extra lipid support. Squalane is non-comedogenic and suitable for all skin types including oily. Unlike most oils, it does not cause breakouts even in combination with Nepal's winter indoor heating that increases sebum viscosity.

Vaseline Original (Rs. 120): The most cost-effective occlusive available in Nepal. For cracked corners of mouth, dry nose edges, and cracked heels — apply a thick layer before sleep. A truly essential Nepal winter product at any budget level.

Lip treatment (Laneige Lip Sleeping Mask, Rs. 2,000 on Daraz): Nepal's winter universally causes chapped lips. Apply every night before sleep. Rinse in morning. Lasts 2–3 months and is the most effective lip treatment available in Nepal's market.

Humidifier (Rs. 2,500–4,000 on Daraz): A bedroom humidifier targeting 45–55% humidity during sleep does more for winter dry skin than any topical product. Eight hours of sleeping in properly humidified air allows the barrier repair cycle to work in conditions that mimic more favourable climates. Single most impactful non-topical winter skin investment in Nepal.

Ingredients to Reduce in Winter

Reduce: strong AHA exfoliants (max twice weekly, not three); benzoyl peroxide (drying in winter, reduce frequency); alcohol-containing toners; physical scrubs; foaming cleansers. Do not stop: retinol (reduce from nightly to every other night maximum; apply moisturiser as buffer before retinol); niacinamide (safe year-round); vitamin C (safe year-round if skin is not irritated); SPF.

Emergency Winter Recovery: When Skin Is Already Damaged

If your skin is already showing winter damage — significant flaking, redness, stinging with most products — implement a 2-week barrier recovery protocol: stop ALL actives (no AHA, BHA, retinol, vitamin C). Use only gentle cleanser + ceramide moisturiser (CeraVe Cream twice daily) + SPF. After 2 weeks of no actives, barrier function typically recovers sufficiently to reintroduce products one at a time starting from gentlest to strongest.

Complete Skincare Guide for All Skin Types

12 min read · Beginner to advanced · All skin types · Nepal tips

Introduction

The single biggest skincare mistake most people make is choosing products for the wrong skin type. A rich ceramide cream ideal for dry skin will cause persistent breakouts on oily skin. A foaming salicylic acid cleanser that works perfectly for oily skin will strip and damage a dry or sensitive barrier. Getting this identification right at the start saves months of frustrating trial and error and wasted money.

This guide walks through every skin type, how to accurately identify yours, the most effective ingredients for each, and the universal principles that apply regardless of skin type. For step-by-step routine structure, read our Morning vs Night Routine guide after identifying your skin type here.

What Causes Different Skin Types?

Skin type is primarily determined by the activity level of your sebaceous glands and the baseline condition of your skin barrier. Genetics set the foundation, but Nepal’s climate, seasonal changes, diet, hormones, stress levels, and product history all significantly influence how your skin behaves day to day.

In Nepal specifically: monsoon humidity (June–September) worsens oiliness for most people; Kathmandu’s cold, dry winters (November–February) damage the barrier and worsen dryness; year-round high-altitude UV accelerates skin ageing for all types and makes hyperpigmentation a persistent challenge.

Step-by-Step: How to Identify Your Skin Type Accurately

1

The Bare-Face Test

Wash your face with a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser. Apply absolutely nothing — no toner, no moisturiser, no serum. Wait exactly 60 minutes in a normal room environment, then examine your skin carefully in natural daylight.

2

Interpret the Results

Visible shine across most of the face: Oily skin.
T-zone shiny, cheeks comfortable or slightly dry: Combination skin (the most common type).
Comfortable, neither shiny nor tight: Normal skin.
Tight, flaky, uncomfortable, or dull: Dry skin.
Any redness, stinging, or immediate reaction: Sensitive skin (which overlaps with any of the above types).

🧷
Oily Skin

Signs: Shine across most of the face within 1–2 hours of washing, enlarged pores, frequent blackheads and breakouts, makeup doesn’t stay. Nepal trigger: Monsoon humidity directly amplifies sebum production. Key ingredients: Salicylic Acid (BHA), Niacinamide 10%, Clay. Avoid: Heavy creams, coconut oil, alcohol toners. Full guide: Best Skincare Routine for Oily Skin in Nepal.

💧
Dry Skin

Signs: Tightness and discomfort after washing, visible flakiness, dull complexion, fine lines more apparent, uncomfortable in cold and low-humidity environments. Nepal trigger: Kathmandu’s cold, dry winters are extremely damaging to dry skin. Key ingredients: Hyaluronic Acid (on damp skin), Ceramides, Squalane, rich Shea-based moisturisers. Avoid: Foaming cleansers, hot water, over-exfoliating (max once a week).

🔀
Combination Skin

Signs: Oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) with normal or dry cheeks. The most common skin type globally. Key ingredients: Niacinamide 10% works across the whole face; lighter gel moisturiser on T-zone, slightly richer cream on cheeks. Strategy: Treat different zones differently rather than applying one product across the entire face.

🌿
Sensitive Skin

Signs: Reacts easily to new products with redness, stinging, or burning. Flushes quickly. Intolerant of fragrances, essential oils, or high-percentage actives. Key ingredients: Centella Asiatica (Cica), Allantoin, Ceramides, Panthenol, Mineral (non-chemical) SPF. Strategy: Minimal ingredient lists, fragrance-free everything, introduce actives one at a time at the lowest possible concentrations.

The Non-Negotiable Universal Rules

1. SPF Every Single Day Without Exception

UV radiation is responsible for 80–90% of visible skin ageing and is the primary cause of hyperpigmentation. At Nepal’s altitude, UV exposure is measurably higher than at sea level and is present year-round — through clouds, through windows, and through haze. Apply SPF 30–50+ every morning as the absolute final skincare step, regardless of skin type, weather, or whether you plan to go outdoors.

2. Double Cleanse Every Single Evening

One cleanser cannot reliably remove SPF and the full day’s pollution particles. In Kathmandu, where air pollution is among the highest in South Asia, unremoved oxidative pollution sitting on your skin overnight accelerates DNA damage and ageing significantly. Oil cleanser first, then your regular cleanser — every night, without exception.

3. Introduce One New Product at a Time

If you add three new products simultaneously and develop a reaction, you will never know which one caused it. Introduce a single new product, wait 2 full weeks to assess skin response, then add the next. This rule applies even to “gentle” products.

4. Patience and Consistency Over Premium Products

Most active ingredients — Vitamin C, Niacinamide, AHAs, Retinol — require 8–16 weeks of consistent daily use to show their full visible effects. Changing products every 3–4 weeks resets the results clock every time. A simple, consistent NPR 1,500 routine used daily for 6 months delivers better skin than a premium 12-product routine used irregularly.

Key Ingredients That Deliver Proven Results

  • Hyaluronic Acid: Draws water into skin. Apply to slightly damp skin and seal immediately with moisturiser. All skin types
  • Niacinamide 10%: Controls oil production, minimises pore appearance, fades dark spots, strengthens the skin barrier. Morning and/or PM. All skin types
  • Vitamin C (Ascorbyl Glucoside): Antioxidant protection, brightening, collagen stimulation. Morning use only, before SPF. Full guide: How to Use Vitamin C Properly
  • Glycolic Acid (AHA): Exfoliates dead skin bonds, resurfaces texture, fades pigmentation. Night use only, 2–3x per week maximum. Never daily
  • Salicylic Acid (BHA): Oil-soluble; penetrates inside pores and clears congestion. Best for oily and acne-prone skin types
  • Ceramides: Rebuild the skin barrier’s natural lipid structure. Essential for dry and sensitive skin. CeraVe and La Roche-Posay are widely available in Nepal

Mistakes to Avoid Regardless of Skin Type

  • Skipping SPF on cloudy days, indoors, or in winter — UV is present and damaging year-round
  • Over-exfoliating — using chemical exfoliants daily destroys the barrier and causes sensitisation
  • Mixing incompatible actives in the same session: Retinol + AHA, very high-concentration Glycolic + Niacinamide
  • Popping or picking pimples — causes permanent scarring and spreads bacteria to adjacent pores
  • Buying unverified products in Nepal — counterfeits are widespread. See our Nepal buying guide before any purchase
  • Expecting visible results in 2 weeks — meaningful change requires consistent daily use for 8–16 weeks minimum

How Nepal’s Seasons Change Your Skin Type Temporarily

An important and widely misunderstood fact: your skin type is not fixed. While your underlying genetics determine your baseline sebum production and barrier function, Nepal’s extreme seasonal variation can temporarily shift your skin’s behaviour significantly. Understanding this prevents the mistake of fighting your moisturiser choice all winter or wondering why your oily skin suddenly feels tight in November.

  • Oily skin in monsoon: Behaves more oily than baseline. Humidity increases sebum production. Normal skin may temporarily behave like oily skin. Treat according to monsoon-oily protocols even if your base type is normal
  • Oily skin in winter: Often becomes “dehydrated oily” — still producing sebum but also feeling tight because the surface layer lacks water. This is very common in Kathmandu winters. The solution is not switching to richer moisturiser (which will cause breakouts) but adding a Hyaluronic Acid serum beneath your existing oil-free moisturiser
  • Dry skin in monsoon: Often feels significantly more comfortable than usual as environmental moisture helps. Do not reduce moisturiser use in monsoon just because skin feels fine — maintaining the routine prevents the rebound dryness that appears when monsoon ends abruptly in October
  • Combination skin year-round: The T-zone/cheek imbalance is most pronounced in winter (dry cheeks, less oily T-zone) and least noticeable in monsoon (both areas more balanced). Year-round Niacinamide 10% is the most effective single-product solution for combination skin in Nepal

Skincare for Nepal’s Specific Skin Concerns

Beyond skin types, several skin concerns are particularly common in Nepal due to the altitude, climate, and lifestyle factors. Here is how to address each within your routine:

  • Hyperpigmentation from sun and acne: Extremely common in Nepal due to high-altitude UV and South Asian melanin patterns. Protocol: SPF 50 PA++++ daily (prevents new pigmentation), Niacinamide 10% (inhibits melanin transfer), Vitamin C AM (brightens), Glycolic Acid PM 2–3x per week (resurfaces). Results in 8–16 weeks of consistent use
  • Winter barrier damage: Kathmandu’s dry, cold, polluted winter air strips the skin barrier repeatedly. Signs: sudden increase in redness, stinging from products that previously caused no reaction, tightness after cleansing. Response: temporarily stop all actives for 1–2 weeks, use only gentle cleanser + ceramide moisturiser until barrier recovers, then reintroduce actives one at a time
  • Pollution-related dullness: Characteristic of Kathmandu residents — skin appears grey and dull despite adequate sleep and hydration. Primary cause is oxidative damage from PM2.5 particles. Response: double cleanse every evening without exception, Vitamin C every morning, exfoliate 2–3x per week PM
  • Altitude adjustment for trekkers: If you are trekking above 3,000m, your skin will experience extreme UV, dryness, and cold simultaneously. Pack: SPF 50+ (reapply every 90 minutes at high altitude), rich barrier cream, lip balm with SPF, and avoid introducing new actives during a trek when skin is already stressed

Conclusion

Excellent skin comes from the right, consistent routine — not from the most expensive products. Identify your skin type accurately, build a simple 5-step core routine using ingredients matched to your specific needs, add one active at a time, and commit for 8–12 weeks before evaluating.
